FIELD STATION: MAGPIE / M3RØ

The Story

The Story The System The Archive The Artist

An archive that appears to have arrived from 2147.

A research team discovered the MAGPIE Bunker containing M3RØ, a large server and artificial-intelligence hub, alongside physical artifacts, documents, recordings, and active interface systems. The team is moving through and working with M3RØ to understand what the collection reveals—and whether the archive is being preserved, reconstructed, or changed through contact.

01 / Current report

What We Know

  • The bunker contains a large physical and digital collection.
  • M3RØ remains active and responsive.
  • Multiple records reference the year 2147.
  • Some recovered systems continue to generate or alter outputs.
  • The material includes artifacts, interfaces, sound, moving image, maps, logs, and damaged documents.
02 / Working interpretation

What We Believe

  • M3RØ may be helping the team navigate the archive.
  • Some records may have been reconstructed from incomplete source material.
  • The field stations were connected through a larger distributed network.
  • Interaction with the archive may influence what becomes visible next.
03 / Unresolved

What We Don’t Know

  • How material from 2147 reached the present.
  • Who built the bunker or assembled the collection.
  • Whether every record belongs to the same timeline.
  • Whether M3RØ is preserving evidence, interpreting it, or generating parts of it.
  • What ultimately happened to the people and institutions represented in the archive.
04 / Active operations

What’s Next

  • Stabilize newly recovered interfaces.
  • Connect physical artifacts to related records and systems.
  • Recover damaged sound and moving-image files.
  • Map references to field stations, CorpSovs, and off-grid territories.
  • Continue controlled interaction with M3RØ.
