MAGPIE treats the future as a consequence of systems already underway—not as an isolated invention.

How did the world of MAGPIE come to exist?

The archive suggests a future formed through automation, state-corporate convergence, climate instability, sovereign infrastructure, and the gradual loss of individualized embodied experience beyond the grid.

WORKING CHRONOLOGY

Key Events

The sequence is provisional. Exact dates remain unresolved, but the archive repeatedly points toward these transitions.

01 / Past

Delegation Era

Automation expands from industrial labor into management, prediction, judgment, and everyday decision-making.

02 / Present

Infrastructure Compacts

Governments increasingly rely on corporations to provide essential technical, logistical, and climate-adaptation systems.

03 / Transition

CorpSov Emergence

Infrastructure providers acquire territorial authority because access to their systems becomes inseparable from citizenship and survival.

04 / Transition

Climate Partition

Mobility, safety, water, energy, and habitable territory become unequally distributed through controlled adaptation zones.

05 / Pre-2147

Grid Consolidation

Identity, work, memory, movement, and social experience become increasingly legible only through networked systems.

06 / Pre-2147

Field Station Era

Counter-networks preserve physical records, local practices, and forms of embodied life that cannot be fully absorbed by the grid.
