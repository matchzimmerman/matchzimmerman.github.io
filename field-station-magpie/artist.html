The Artist

The Story The System The Archive The Artist

Match Zimmerman is both the builder of MAGPIE and one of its investigators.

FIELD STATION: MAGPIE is a speculative archival practice spanning physical artifacts, installation, painting, sound, moving image, procedural code, narrative, and human–AI systems. Zimmerman works as a speculative archivist: constructing the archive while also approaching it as a researcher whose interpretations remain partial, provisional, and subject to revision.

01 / Role

Artist and Speculative Archivist

Zimmerman authors the world, designs its systems, fabricates its objects, and organizes its records. At the same time, the project is structured so that procedural outputs, material accidents, automated processes, and collaborative interpretation can produce evidence he did not fully predetermine.

02 / Practice

Across Media

The work moves between physical artifacts, painted interfaces, tarps, books, sound, animation, video, code-generated systems, exhibitions, and public archival tools. No single medium contains the project; each functions as another recovered fragment.

03 / Method

Human–AI Collaboration

Human judgment, authorship, and material practice are combined with procedural systems and artificial agents. These tools are not presented as autonomous creators. They operate within a directed research and production framework designed, evaluated, and revised by the artist.

04 / Position

Inside and Outside the Fiction

The artist occupies two positions at once: the real-world creator of MAGPIE and an in-world researcher studying an archive larger than he fully understands. The tension between those roles is part of the work rather than something the project attempts to conceal.
