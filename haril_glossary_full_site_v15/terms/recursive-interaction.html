Recursive Interaction

Looped engagement for alignment

Definition

A structured mode of engagement where human and AI loop repeatedly through inquiry and response, refining output with each cycle until alignment emerges. Unlike repetition, recursion compresses noise and surfaces signal.

What it looks like

Returning to a prompt multiple times, each round sharpening clarity, structure, and intent.

Overlaps

Relates to Recursive Prompt Architecture (RPA) and Compression Threshold; supports Iterative Convergence.

Importance

Establishes recursion as the foundation of human–AI alignment — the engine behind refinement and convergence.
